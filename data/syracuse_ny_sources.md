# Syracuse, NY Source Notes

## Geography

- Primary geography: Syracuse city limits, Onondaga County, NY.
- Population and density: city limits. Density is calculated from Census QuickFacts July 1, 2025 population estimate, 144,896, divided by 2020 land area, 25.06 square miles, yielding about 5,782 people per square mile.
- Housing: Zillow city-level ZHVI for Syracuse, NY, data through 2026-05-31.
- Cost of living: ERI city cost-of-living estimate says Syracuse is 2 percent less expensive than the national average; stored as index 98.
- Sales tax: combined Syracuse / Onondaga County rate of 8.00 percent.
- Income tax: New York top individual income-tax rate, 10.90 percent.
- VA access: Syracuse VA Medical Center, 800 Irving Avenue. Distance is approximate driving distance from Syracuse City Hall to the medical center, rounded for display.
- Elections: Onondaga County presidential returns. The 2016 county values were cross-checked against New York county-result tables sourced to the New York State Board of Elections; 2024 values use New York State Board of Elections certified county results. Percent fields are two-party winner share, rounded to whole percent. Trend fields use two-party share: Republican share fell from about 42.7 percent in 2016 to about 41.4 percent in 2024, so `rep_vote_share_change_pp = -1.3` and `dem_vote_share_change_pp = 1.3`.
- Crime: `TCI` is a violent-crime-rate proxy indexed to the 2024 FBI national violent-crime rate. OpenCrime reports Syracuse's 2024 violent crime rate as 765.3 per 100,000, based on FBI city data. FBI's 2024 national rate is 359.1 per 100,000, so 765.3 / 359.1 * 100 = 213.1, rounded to 213.
- Weather: annual rain, snow, sun days, January low, and July high use BestPlaces/WeatherSpark climate summaries for Syracuse and Syracuse Hancock International Airport; July humidity uses Weather-US monthly humidity values.
- LGBTQ score: HRC 2025 Municipal Equality Index final score, 100. State policy score uses MAP New York overall score, 44.5/49.
- Economic hubs: `tech_hub=Y` because Syracuse is part of the Central New York semiconductor corridor and the city runs Syracuse Surge as a technology/workforce strategy. `defense_hub=Y` because Hancock Field Air National Guard Base and the 174th Attack Wing are in the Syracuse area.
