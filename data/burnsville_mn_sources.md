# Burnsville, MN Source Notes

Retrieval date: 2026-07-10.

## Geography

- Primary geography: Burnsville city limits, Dakota County, MN.
- Population and density: Census QuickFacts July 1, 2025 population estimate, 65,244. Density is calculated from that estimate divided by 2020 land area, 24.94 square miles, yielding about 2,616 people per square mile. Census QuickFacts also reports 2020 density of 2,579.2 people per square mile.
- Housing: Zillow city-level ZHVI for Burnsville, MN, updated 2026-05-31.
- Cost of living: PayScale says Burnsville's cost of living is 8 percent lower than the national average; stored as index 92.
- Sales tax: Burnsville general combined rate stored as 8.125 percent. Minnesota's state rate is 6.875 percent; local/special components include the metro area and Dakota County transportation taxes. The Minnesota Department of Revenue rate calculator is the official location lookup, and Avalara/Taxwire were used as address-level cross-checks for Burnsville.
- Income tax: Minnesota's top 2026 individual income-tax rate is 9.85 percent.
- VA access: Minneapolis VA Medical Center, One Veterans Drive, Minneapolis. Distance is approximate driving distance from Burnsville City Hall to the medical center, rounded for display.
- Elections: Burnsville city precinct rows from Minnesota Secretary of State federal/state precinct spreadsheets. Percent fields are two-party winner share, rounded to whole percent. Trend fields use two-party share: Democratic share rose from about 56.4 percent in 2016 to about 60.7 percent in 2024, so `rep_vote_share_change_pp = -4.3` and `dem_vote_share_change_pp = 4.3`. Because this is city-level precinct evidence, `city_politics` is stored as `Liberal`.
- Crime: `TCI` is a violent-crime-rate proxy indexed to the 2024 FBI national violent-crime rate. OpenCrime reports Burnsville's 2024 violent crime rate as 209.7 per 100,000. Using FBI's 2024 national rate of 359.1 per 100,000 gives about 58.
- Weather: annual rain, snow, sunny days, January low, and July high use BestPlaces Burnsville climate summaries. July humidity uses a secondary climate summary because NOAA-style relative humidity was not quickly available in the same page format; climate categorization is unaffected because Burnsville meets the cold/snowy rule.
- LGBTQ score: Burnsville was not located in HRC's 2025 MEI city list. MAP gives Minnesota an overall LGBTQ policy score of 36.75/49, rated High. Stored `lgbtq_rating` converts that state policy score to a 0-100 proxy, 75, while leaving `lgbtq_mei_score` blank.
- Economic hubs: `tech_hub=N` and `defense_hub=N`; Burnsville benefits from the broader Twin Cities economy but does not itself have a major tech cluster or active-duty defense installation.
