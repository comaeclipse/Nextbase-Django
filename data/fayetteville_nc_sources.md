# Fayetteville, NC Source Notes

Retrieved July 9, 2026. The city row uses Fayetteville city data where available. Presidential results and the political label use Cumberland County because the official election files are county/precinct records rather than a citywide municipal tally.

## Field choices

- Population and density: 209,120 (July 1, 2025 estimate) and 1,406.3 people per square mile (2020) from Census QuickFacts; density is rounded to 1,406.
- Housing and costs: Zillow's city-level typical home value was $227,271 (data through May 31, 2026). Cumberland County's combined sales-tax rate is 7%; North Carolina's individual income-tax rate for tax years after 2025 is 3.99%. RentCafe reported Fayetteville's cost of living as 7% below the national average, stored as index 93.
- Veteran access: Fayetteville VA Medical Center is in the city at 2300 Ramsey Street, so the row records 0 miles. The state-benefits text is restricted to benefits documented by North Carolina DMVA, including the qualifying $45,000 property-tax exclusion.
- Politics: 2016 precinct returns were summed for all Cumberland County US PRESIDENT records: Clinton 71,605 and Trump 51,265 (Clinton 58.28% two-party). The certified 2024 Cumberland totals were Harris 78,631 and Trump 59,840 (Harris 56.78% two-party). Republican two-party share therefore increased 1.49 percentage points. The `County-level: Liberal` label preserves this geography qualifier.
- Crime: North Carolina SBI reported 1,427 violent offenses in Fayetteville for 2024. TCI is an open proxy: 1,427 / 209,120 x 100,000 = 682 violent offenses per 100,000, divided by the FBI 2024 national violent-crime rate of 359.1 and multiplied by 100, rounded to 190. It mixes the nearest available population vintage and should be read as an approximate comparison, not a municipal FBI rate.
- Climate: NOAA/NCEI 1991-2020 normals for Fayetteville Regional Airport (USW00093740) supply 44.58 inches of annual precipitation, a 34.0 F January normal low, and a 91.4 F July normal high. Snow is the 1991-2020 Fayetteville normal from the NC State Climate Office; sunny days are from the City FY26 budget; July humidity is from Timeanddate station climate history. Values are rounded for the product.
- LGBTQ and marijuana: HRC's 2025 Municipal Equality Index scorecard gives Fayetteville 50; MAP gives North Carolina 6.25/49. North Carolina DHHS says the state has not enacted comprehensive medical or adult-use legalization, outside the separate tribal-law context in the Qualla Boundary.
- Defense hub is supported by Fayetteville's adjacency to Fort Liberty. Tech hub is intentionally left unset rather than inferred.
